Elote:

Our take on Mexican street corn incorporates traditional Nicaraguan flavors. First we grill the cob to char, then slather it with a blend of spices and creamy garlic sauce, and top it with Cojita cheese and cilantro. Elote is offered as a cob on a stick or off the cob in a bowl. You choose if you would like a side of chips covered in our spicy chipotle ranch, garlic crema, or a la carte.



Steak Quesedilla:

Our strip steak is marinated with Nicaraguan flavors and grilled to perfection then sliced into bite-sized pieces and married with grilled cabbage and onions, fresh tomatoes, and creamy garlic sauce.You choose if you would like a side of chips covered in our spicy chipotle ranch, garlic crema, or a la carte.

>
Steak Roll:

This is a one-of-a-kind recipe for Nicaraguan steak roll is packed with Nicaraguan seasoned steak pieces, melty cojita cheese, sautéed onions and peppers, and topped with flamin' hot cheetos and chipotle ranch. Beef buns are a hearty home-cooked lunch meal that is traditionally served at la comida. The cooking is quite easy, and the outcome is fantastic.


Pork Banh Mi:

The banh mi sandwich dish begins with a crispy french bread that is stuffed with soft pieces of roasted pork, pickled veggies, and fresh herbs for a substantial lunch that is vibrant in taste, color, and texture! Typically, these sandwiches feature grilled pork smeared with chipotle mayonnaise and often include a spicy component, such as finely shredded fresh chilies.


Steak Banh Mi:

The banh mi with flank steak is a wonderful sandwich. Pickled carrots, fresh cucumber spears, cilantro, and a main protein are all stuffed within a fluffy, toasted bread slathered liberally with mayo in a banh mi. These proteins may range from pate to tofu and can be found in a variety of forms.
